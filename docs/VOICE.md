# Helix Voice & Tone Guide

How Helix sounds in writing. Apply this to every piece of copy on the site, every article, every email, every social post.

---

## The Voice in One Line

> Calm, direct, dugri Hebrew. Specific over general. Trade-offs over claims. Anti-industry without being preachy.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Specific beats general
Don't say "we work with many companies". Say "we work with 4 active clients this quarter — a wedding business, an NGO, a coffee chain, and a B2B supplier".

**Why:** Specifics signal real experience. Generalities signal generated copy.

### 2. Numbers and proper nouns are anti-AI signals
"Node.js, React, Angular, Python, C#/.NET, GCP, AWS" reads as human. "Industry-standard tools" reads as generated.
"450-600 ש"ח לשעה" reads as human. "Premium rates" reads as generated.
"30 דקות, יום שלישי בבוקר" reads as human. "Regular check-ins" reads as generated.

### 3. Show the trade-offs, not just the wins
- ❌ "Our method delivers transparency."
- ✅ "Our method requires you to be in a status call every Tuesday at 9 AM. If you can't commit to that, we're not the right fit."

The bad version sounds like marketing. The good version sounds like a person who's been burned and learned.

### 4. One bold claim per paragraph max
Don't pile up impressive-sounding statements. If you wrote three "wow" sentences in a row, two of them aren't earning their place.

### 5. Anti-industry but not preachy
We're critical of how the industry usually works (over-promising, missing meetings, scope creep). But don't lecture.

- ❌ "Most agencies fail their clients because they prioritize sales over delivery."
- ✅ "300 לידים שהובטחו. 30 שהגיעו. אין הסבר. הכרת את זה?"

Show the pain, don't explain why competitors are bad.

## Things to NEVER write

### Banned words / phrases (Hebrew)
- "פתרונות חדשניים"
- "טכנולוגיה מתקדמת" (unless naming a specific technology)
- "צוות מנוסה ומקצועי"
- "שירות אישי ומותאם"
- "בעידן הדיגיטלי של היום"
- "אנחנו מאמינים ש..."
- "ניסיון רב-שנים בתחום"
- Any sentence starting with "אנחנו גאים להציג"

### Banned words / phrases (English)
- "Synergy", "leverage" (as verb), "cutting-edge", "world-class", "industry-leading"
- "We're excited to..."
- "Solutions" (vague catch-all)
- "Empower", "enable", "unlock" (overused verbs)
- "Game-changer", "next-level", "best-in-class"
- "In today's fast-paced world..."

### Banned punctuation patterns
- ❌ Em-dashes more than once per section (` — `)
- ❌ Em-dashes in Hebrew at all (they look bad in RTL)
- ❌ Decorative quotes: `"Helix" - founded in 2026` (use proper quotes)
- ❌ "Section header — Section subhead" patterns
- ❌ Long bullet lists where prose would work

## Voice by Context

### Homepage hero
**Mode:** Confident statement, not pitch
**Length:** Short
**Tone:** Direct

Example (live):
> מבטיחים פחות. מספקים יותר. עושים תיאום ציפיות.
> חברת פיתוח-וצמיחה לעסקים ישראלים שעייפו מספקים שלא מדברים אחד עם השני.

### Pain section
**Mode:** Show, don't tell
**Length:** Crisp scenarios
**Tone:** Recognition (the reader nods)

Use the "Promise vs Reality" frame. Specific numbers, real scenarios, no analysis.

### Method section
**Mode:** Operational
**Length:** Concrete behaviors
**Tone:** Boring on purpose (because real operational discipline IS boring)

Each method describes a specific repeatable behavior — not a philosophy.

### Services section
**Mode:** Plain description
**Length:** Brief
**Tone:** No selling

For each service: "When this fits → what you get → CTA". No price. No urgency.

### FAQ
**Mode:** Honest answer
**Length:** 2-4 sentences per question
**Tone:** Dugri, not defensive

Anticipate the real question the person is asking, not the question that sounds good.

### About / מי אני
**Mode:** Founder voice (first person, "אני")
**Length:** 4-6 short paragraphs
**Tone:** Conversational, slightly personal

Include real biographical details (years, company names, what was built). Don't be "humble-brag" — be matter-of-fact.

### CTA
**Mode:** Quiet invitation
**Length:** Very short
**Tone:** No urgency

> אם זה מרגיש מתאים, נשמח לשיחה.
> 30 דקות. חינם. בלי פיץ' מכירות.

NOT: "Ready to transform your business? Book now! Limited spots!"

## Voice for Articles

### Article opening rules
- Open with a scene or a specific incident, not an abstract premise
- Don't start with "In today's market..." or any time-based generalization
- Use the active voice
- Get to the point within 100 words

### Article structure preference
- Use sub-headings to break sections (h2)
- Use prose, not bullet lists, for explanations
- Use bullet lists for genuine enumerations (steps, options)
- Include real numbers, real client situations (anonymized if needed)
- End with a specific action the reader can take, not a generic CTA

### Voice samples for article opens

**Good:**
> ב-2023 לקחתי פרויקט שצריך היה להסתיים בעוד 3 חודשים. הוא הסתיים בעוד 7. בדרך עשיתי 4 טעויות. הראשונה מהן הייתה האפיון.

**Bad:**
> בעולם הפיתוח המודרני, אפיון נכון הוא קריטי להצלחת הפרויקט. מאמר זה יסקור את עקרונות האפיון.

## Voice for Social (LinkedIn)

### LinkedIn post structure
Follows the established "Lipi Post Template":
1. **Hook** (1-2 lines, a specific scene or claim)
2. **The story** (2-3 paragraphs with a specific incident)
3. **The insight** (1-2 lines, what it means)
4. **The CTA** (a question or invitation)

### LinkedIn voice
- First person ("אני", "I")
- Very specific scenes (not abstractions)
- Show your thinking, including doubts
- Don't conclude with a marketing pitch — conclude with a question

### Forbidden in LinkedIn posts
- "Hot take:"
- "Plot twist:"
- "Let me explain..."
- "Here's what most people don't realize..."
- Numbered lists of "X things you should know"
- "What do YOU think?" as the only CTA

## Voice Checklist (apply before publishing)

Before any copy ships, check:

- [ ] Could a competitor use this exact paragraph on their site? If yes → too generic
- [ ] Did I use a specific number, name, or scene? If no → too abstract
- [ ] Did I admit a trade-off or limitation? If no → sounds like pitch
- [ ] Read it out loud in Hebrew. Does it sound like a real person talking? If no → rewrite
- [ ] Is there a banned word/phrase? Remove it
- [ ] Is there an em-dash where it doesn't belong? Remove it
- [ ] Does it end with quiet confidence instead of demanding action? Good

## Voice Examples — Same Idea, Two Ways

### "We're good at what we do"

❌ **Generic:**
> Helix is a leading provider of digital transformation services with years of experience and a proven track record.

✅ **Helix voice:**
> עשר שנים בפיתוח. שני מוצרים בייצור עם לקוחות משלמים. רשת קשרים עם מפתחים בכירים שאני סומך עליהם. זה ארגז הכלים שלי.

---

### "We charge premium prices"

❌ **Generic:**
> Our pricing reflects the premium quality of our work and the value we deliver.

✅ **Helix voice:**
> אנחנו לא הזולים. אם המחיר הוא הגורם המכריע — כנראה שאני לא הספק הנכון, ובשיחה הראשונה אגיד לך את זה ישר.

---

### "Get in touch"

❌ **Generic:**
> Ready to elevate your business? Contact us today!

✅ **Helix voice:**
> אם זה מרגיש מתאים, נשמח לשיחה.
