## The Problem We Solve

The Israeli small/medium business owner typically buys product development from one vendor and marketing services from another. These vendors don't communicate with each other. The result:

- **Marketing over-promises** ("300 leads at 8₪ each") and under-delivers ("you got 30 leads at 60₪")
- **Product development goes off-rails** ("MVP in 3 months" becomes "7 months and half-built")
- **Communication breaks down** (weekly calls promised, never happen, customer becomes the chaser)

We solve this by being the single brain that owns both sides. Product and marketing under one roof, with operational discipline (weekly status calls, written scope documents, real-number reporting).

## Pricing Strategy

### Public Position: NO PRICES on the website
This is a deliberate strategic decision, not an oversight.

**Reasoning:**
1. Prices on the website anchor clients before they understand the value
2. Each project is genuinely different — no fixed price fits all
3. Premium consulting brands (McKinsey, BCG, Aviv Ben-Yosef) don't publish prices
4. The brand promise is "expectation alignment together" — price is part of that alignment

### Where prices appear
- ✅ FAQ section: general ranges (15-25K, 30-90K, 25-45K/mo)
- ✅ Scoping meeting: specific price for specific scope
- ❌ Service cards: no prices
- ❌ Hero / CTAs: no "starting at" prices
- ❌ Ads: no prices

### Pricing philosophy in client conversations
- Value-based, not hours-based
- Always present scope and price together (never one without the other)
- Include "exit terms" upfront (30-day notice on retainer, fixed price on projects)
- Transparent about what's in / what's not

## Differentiation

What makes Helix different from competitors:

### 1. The "No Handoff" Promise
Most engagements involve at least 2 vendors (dev shop + marketing agency) who don't talk. Helix is one entity handling both. Same brain → same context → faster iteration → fewer "that's not my problem" moments.

### 2. Weekly Status Discipline
Every Tuesday at 9:00 AM, 30-minute documented status call. Always. This sounds simple but is genuinely rare in the industry.

### 3. Written Scope Before Money
Every project starts with a documented scope agreement. Signed by both sides. This is the antidote to scope creep and "but we said".

### 4. Honest Forecasts
No "300 leads for 8₪ each" promises. We tell you what's likely, with confidence intervals, and we explain when our forecasts are off (because they will be sometimes).

### 5. Single Accountability
On any project, Eran is personally accountable. He may use trusted freelancers for capacity, but they work under Helix and his responsibility — the client deals with one person.
